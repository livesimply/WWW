Papers

  • The value of trust: pricing utility tokens in a blockchain-based network
  • Expected reward optimization with state-specific controls
  • Optimal Pricing in discrete choice models (accepted at INFORMS Revenue Management & Pricing Conference 2019, Stanford, USA)
    • We derive optimal pricing for logit discrete choice models in closed form
  • Pricing a Digital Services Marketplace (accepted at International Conference on Web Services 2019, San Diego, USA)
    • We derive optimal pricing under imperfect information that exploits partially-observable heterogeneity in the marketplace supply.
  • Anatomy of a Service Marketplace (work-in-progress)
    • Rather than view the marketplace as a black box that matches demand and supply, in this paper we take a market microstructure approach and explicitly model the matching and operating rules of the marketplace to determine the laws of marketplace evolution and the effectiveness of the matching process. We estimate the model using the rich dataset.
  • A Comparative Study of Query-biased and Non-redundant Snippets for Structured Search on Mobile Devices (published at ACM CIKM Conference on Information and Knowledge Management 2016, Indianapolis, USA)
  • Relevance-aware Filtering of Tuples Sorted by an Attribute Value via Direct Optimization of Search Quality Metrics (published at ACM SIGIR Conference on Research and Development in Information Retrieval 2015, Santiago, Chile)
  • Responsible Capacity Development (accepted at MSOM Manufacturing and Service Operations Conference 2013, Fontainebleau, France)
    • A buyer-supplier relationship is a tool that can incentivize investments in responsible production by the supplier. The paper describes how to choose optimal relationship parameters -- commitment horizon and price terms, so that the responsible investments are maximized. In certain cases, surprisingly, a low-commitment relationship yields both higher profits and responsibility investments.
